Indoor Putting Practice Drills

Published by IndoorGolfFinders.com • Indoor Golf Guides

Putting accounts for nearly 40 percent of your golf score, yet most golfers spend less than 10 percent of their practice time on the green. When winter weather or time constraints keep you off the course, indoor putting practice becomes your secret weapon for dropping strokes. The good news: modern indoor golf venues offer serious putting-focused environments where you can drill specific games and build consistency year round.

What to Look For

When you're hunting for a venue that lets you focus on putting drills, the simulator brand matters less than the putting green setup itself. Some venues feature full 18 hole layouts, while others dedicate space to dedicated putting areas with real greens or high quality synthetic surfaces. The best venues let you practice isolated putting without playing a full course.

Look for venues equipped with accurate green reading technology. The better simulators like TrackMan, Full Swing, Foresight GCQuad, SkyTrak, and Bushnell Launch Pro all track putt speed, break, and distance. This data feedback is crucial because you need to know whether you missed a putt because of poor aim or poor speed control. A great venue will display this information clearly on your screen between shots.

Course selection matters too. Can you run the same putting drill on multiple course greens? Some venues let you customize hole sequences or practice from the same distance repeatedly, which is essential for developing confidence in specific situations like three footers or breaking putts from eight feet.

Instruction options separate solid venues from mediocre ones. Ask whether the staff can offer putting specific coaching. Even a 15 minute session reviewing your stroke mechanics and green reading approach will accelerate improvement. Some venues bundle lesson packages with hourly rates, which often represents better value than paying separately.

Finally, evaluate the booking process. Can you reserve your preferred time slot online, or are you fighting through phone calls and availability gaps? Easy booking means you'll actually show up consistently, and consistency beats talent for putting practice.

What It Costs

Expect to pay between 30 and 60 dollars per hour at most indoor golf venues, with pricing depending heavily on location and simulator technology. Major metropolitan areas typically run toward the higher end, while suburban or secondary markets often land in the 30 to 40 dollar range.

Many venues offer membership programs that reduce hourly rates to 20 to 25 dollars if you commit to a monthly fee. If you plan to practice putting weekly or more, memberships make financial sense and lock in predictable costs. Some clubs also run league nights and special events at discounted rates, so ask what's available beyond standard hourly bookings.

Day passes occasionally appear as an option, letting you try a venue before committing. These typically run 50 to 75 dollars for a few hours of access, which helps you decide whether the facility and instruction quality justify membership.

Good value means paying for simulator accuracy and instruction quality, not fancy aesthetics. A clean room with reliable technology and knowledgeable staff is worth more than a venue with a flashy bar setup if your goal is serious putting improvement.

Tips for Getting the Most Out of It

Book during off peak hours like weekday mornings or early afternoons to secure lower rates and uninterrupted practice time. You'll concentrate better without a crowd, and staff have more availability to offer coaching tips.

Bring your own putter. Most venues welcome this, and using your competition putter during practice builds muscle memory for tournament play. You'll also confirm whether your stroke and setup match your equipment.

Request swing or putting analysis during your session. Ask the staff to review your mechanics on screen and identify specific patterns in your misses. Are you leaving putts short? Pushing them right? Understanding your tendencies transforms practice from mindless repetition into targeted improvement.

Run structured drills rather than just playing random holes. Practice the same distance until you make five in a row. Work on specific break angles. Simulate pressure situations like making the same putt from six feet three times consecutively. Drills with clear pass or fail criteria keep you accountable.

Ask about lesson packages combining multiple sessions. Instructors often discount bundled lessons, and weekly coaching creates measurable progress over a month or two.
